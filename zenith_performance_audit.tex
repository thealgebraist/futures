\section{Introduction}
Project Zenith represents the culmination of the futures analysis project, transforming the predictive research into a production-grade Short-Only trading engine. The core innovation is the integration of a GARCH(1,1) volatility gating mechanism to filter out signals in high-entropy regimes.

\section{GARCH-FFNN Hybrid Architecture}
The engine utilizes a recursive GARCH model to estimate real-time volatility:
\begin{equation}
\sigma_t^2 = \omega + \alpha r_{t-1}^2 + \beta \sigma_{t-1}^2
\end{equation}
Trading signals from the 512-neuron FFNN are only executed when $\sigma_t$ is below $1.5 \times$ the historical mean, effectively implementing a "Dynamic Risk-Off" protocol.

\section{Formal Risk Bounds}
The capital preservation logic was verified in Coq. The \texttt{zenith\_capital\_preservation} theorem proves that for any positive return $r$, the portfolio value $V$ remains static if the estimated volatility exceeds the safety threshold, preventing ruinous drawdowns during regime shifts.
